Jose Joya

A pioneer of Abstract Expressionism
Held as a National Artist in 2003

Jose Joya (1931-1995) was a Filipino artist known for his abstract paintings and sculptures that often featured bold colors and geometric shapes. He was a leading figure in Philippine modern art and served as a professor and dean at the University of the Philippines College of Fine Arts.

The painting depicts a traditional Torogan, a type of house found in the Maranao culture of Mindanao in the southern Philippines.

The Torogan is a longhouse characterized by its steeply pitched roof, intricate carvings, and distinctive panolong, or crest, which symbolizes the rank and prestige of the family who owns the house. In Joya's painting, the Torogan is depicted in vibrant colors, with bold, abstract shapes that evoke the ornate carvings and designs found on the actual houses.

Achievements of Jose Joya

  • National Artist of the Philippines: In 2003, Joya was declared a National Artist of the Philippines for Visual Arts, in recognition of his significant contributions to the country's cultural heritage.
  • Founder of the Philippine Art Gallery: Joya founded the Philippine Art Gallery in 1961, which was the first commercial art gallery in the Philippines. The gallery played a crucial role in promoting modern art in the country and showcasing the works of Filipino artists.
  • Exhibitions in international venues: Joya's works have been exhibited in prestigious venues around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York, the Art Institute of Chicago, and the Centre Georges Pompidou in Paris.
  • Development of abstract expressionism in the Philippines: Joya is widely recognized as one of the pioneers of abstract expressionism in the Philippines. His innovative use of color and form helped to push the boundaries of traditional Filipino art and paved the way for a new generation of modern artists.
  • Awards and recognition: Joya received numerous awards and recognition throughout his career, including the Republic Cultural Heritage Award in 1960, the Patnubay ng Sining at Kalinangan Award in 1970, and the Order of National Artists in 2003.
